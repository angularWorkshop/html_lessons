The Rise of the World Wide Web

The Beginning

The WWW was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 as a system for information management at CERN. It revolutionized how humans share knowledge.

The First Proposal

"This proposal concerns the management of general information about accelerators and experiments at CERN."

— Tim Berners-Lee, 1989

How It Works

At its core, the web uses HTML to create documents connected by hyperlinks. Every web page you visit is an HTML document rendered by your browser.

Impact on Society

The web has democratized access to information for billions of people around the world. It transformed education, commerce, and communication forever.
