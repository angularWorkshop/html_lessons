The Art of Web Typography

Published on January 15, 2024 · 8 min read

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed.

Why Typography Matters

Good typography establishes a strong visual hierarchy, provides a graphic balance to the website, and sets the product's overall tone. Typography should guide and inform your users, optimize readability and accessibility, and ensure an excellent user experience.

When we talk about web typography, we refer to the practice of selecting and applying fonts, adjusting spacing, and creating layouts that enhance the reading experience on screen.

"Typography is two-dimensional architecture, based on experience and imagination, and guided by rules and readability."

— Hermann Zapf

Key Principles

1. Visual Hierarchy

Create clear distinctions between heading levels using size, weight, and spacing.

2. Line Length

The ideal line length for body text is 45-75 characters per line.

3. Line Height

Body text typically needs a line-height between 1.4 and 1.8.

Font Pairing

A classic pairing combines a serif font for headings with a sans-serif for body text.

  • Playfair Display + System UI
  • Georgia + Helvetica
  • Merriweather + Open Sans
  • Lora + Roboto
